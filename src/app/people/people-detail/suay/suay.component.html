Tester

Why do you choose to be a tester?

When I was a student, all students had the same basic classes and studied all together. When my friends finished their applications, they would call me up to try it out and I would end up finding bugs with the programs which helped them identify and resolve them. Since then, my friends would always call me up to test out their programs before submitting them to professors and that’s how I developed the passion to be a tester. I decided that I will be a tester, but I got some negative feedbacks. Others advised that you have better growth potential in software development field and I am a skilled programmer but I believe if everyone is developing, who will be testing? No one will be in charge of quality assurance. So I choose to be a tester. Being a tester, we have to really focus on the customer’s needs and user experience. We want to make sure that there is no problem when the customers use our product.

Which characteristic would you like to remove from yourself?

I want to remove my fear and wariness. I think having some fear is okay because it helps us plan better and consider actions before doing something. However, fear is always limiting and it holds me back from being the best I can be. An example is when we talk or deal with customers, I usually worry about whether we can handle the issues or satisfy their needs (In the end, we mostly do well and they are usually happy, though). Fear affects our body, mind and spirit. I want to always do my best. If we worry about fear, we cannot improve ourselves. I don’t want to worry about if I will reach my goals in life.

What kind of people do you like?

I like people who are kind, responsible and considerate. I love people who take care of one another. I guess I am that kind of person, so it’s good to be around people who are also like that. If we take a moment to be kind and take care of other people, we will all feel good and keep going in life. Oh, but if it’s related to work, I love people with creative solutions. If I have a product and they find some better way of doing something, I feel happy to be working with them. I love the creativity of people in the workplace.

What kind of people would you like to be?

I like who I am. But I hope to reduce my fear. Especially during presentation, I feel so vulnerable because I’m so worried about not doing well. I think I’m getting better. In the next 10 years, I want to be in a place where people trust my opinion and my skills due to my expertise and experience because I’m no longer inexperienced. And, of course, being able to present projects and ideas without fear.
